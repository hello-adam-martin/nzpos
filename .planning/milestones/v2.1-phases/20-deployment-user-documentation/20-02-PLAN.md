**Section 7: GST and Pricing (H2) — USER-02**
Per D-11 (dedicated section within this file), D-12 (plain English, no formulas, worked examples), D-13 (legal disclaimer).

Opening: "All prices in NZPOS include GST. When you enter a price, that's what your customer pays — NZPOS works out the GST automatically."

**Worked example 1 — no discount:**
```
Product: Cleaning spray
Price you enter: $23.00 (this is what the customer pays)

NZPOS automatically calculates:
  GST (15%): $3.00
  Price before GST: $20.00

On the receipt, your customer sees:
  Subtotal: $20.00
  GST (15%): $3.00
  Total: $23.00
```

**Worked example 2 — with discount (per D-12):**
```
Product: Cleaning spray ($23.00)
You apply a $5.00 discount

The customer pays: $18.00
NZPOS calculates GST on the discounted amount:
  GST (15%): $2.35
  Price before GST: $15.65
  Total charged: $18.00

This is correct — IRD requires GST to be calculated on
the amount actually charged, not the original price.
```

**Why this matters paragraph:** Brief explanation that IRD requires GST on actual sale amount (post-discount). NZPOS does this automatically.

**Legal disclaimer per D-13:** Blockquote:
> NZPOS calculates GST automatically following IRD guidelines, but this is not tax advice. Consult your accountant for your specific obligations, including whether you are required to register for GST.
